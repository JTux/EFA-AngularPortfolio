Ever since I was a child I've been working on creating things. While most of that involved paper on pencil, I would occasionally dabble with at creating something with my hands. For example, at age four I created a paper helicopter with moving rotors. I can't say I ever had an intended career path in mind, but the idea of being an author or artist were never far from my mind. If I wasn't writing, drawing, or reading, then I'd probably be playing with LEGO. This inspired a desire to go into engineering and while I did not pursue that, I'd say having found my way to a point where I can express myself creatively while still working with technology is a wonderful middle ground. All while helping others? Sign me up!

Growing up I was fortunate enough to have what felt like a decent amount of exposure to technology. While I can't say I was one of the lucky ones who has been programming since a young age, I definitely did find myself playing with technology whenever I was able to get my hands on it. I was able to dabble in high school in classes such as web design, A+ computer repair, and robotics engineering. In my spare time I would try my hand at writing small scripts for video games whenever I could, basically anything that allowed me to express some level of creativity through technology.

While I often found myself capable of excelling in traditional education, I also found myself unable to find the motivation. When it comes up I usually tell people that dropping out of college was the best thing I did. While I really do love learning, it happened to be the wrong time for me. A few years later when I decided it was time to go back, I found out about Eleven Fifty. I had spent some of my time working on learning some programming and figured this would be a good middle ground. While attending Eleven Fifty as a student in the middle of 2018 I found it really was my calling. I also found myself helping my classmates. When I found I could turn this into a career I jumped at the opportunity.

While some people treat this field as just a job, I find that it's so much more. When asked the question "What do you do in your free time?" sure I can answer a handful of different things, but there's nothing quite as satisfying as working with code. It's creative, it's logic, it's a lot of problem solving. Programming is definitely a passion. While it's not for everyone, it's definitely for me.
